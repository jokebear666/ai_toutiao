  • [arXiv260105] Symmetric Submodular Functions, Uncrossable Functions, and Structural Submodularity

    • tags: [other], [combinatorial optimization], [structural submodularity, pliable family, uncrossable family, symmetric submodular function]

    • authors: Miles Simmons, Ishan Bansal, Joe Cheriyan

    • institution: University of Waterloo, Cornell University, Amazon

    • link: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2601.00140

    • contributions: 1. Introduces and formalizes the concept of a pliable family of sets satisfying structural submodularity, connecting it to prior work on abstract separation systems and uncrossable families. 2. Constructs, for any integer d≥2, a specific pliable family that satisfies structural submodularity. 3. Proves that this constructed family is not representable as the sublevel set of any symmetric submodular function and cannot be partitioned into d or fewer uncrossable families.

    • thumbnail: https://pub-9ba4a5dae3bf4fc7b7d26411a74e92db.r2.dev/thumbnails/4d4ff315217b1c23035f98624aa84f760d45efb2a256772c669208d4c8e0e7c5_w640_q70.webp

    • Simple LLM Summary: This paper studies combinatorial properties of set families, specifically pliable families that satisfy structural submodularity. It constructs a counterexample family to show that not every such family can be represented by a symmetric submodular function. Furthermore, it proves this family is complex in that it cannot be decomposed into a small number of simpler uncrossable families.

  • [arXiv260105] Solution to a problem on isolation of cliques in uniform hypergraphs

    • tags: [other], [extremal combinatorics], [hypergraph isolation, clique isolation number, extremal structures, closed neighbourhood]

    • authors: Peter Borg

    • institution: University of Malta

    • link: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2601.00104

    • contributions: 1. Proves an upper bound for the KkrK_k^rKkr​-isolation number in connected rrr-uniform hypergraphs, showing it is at most n/(k+1)n/(k+1)n/(k+1) except for specific extremal cases. 2. Solves a recent open problem posed by Li, Zhang and Ye regarding clique isolation in hypergraphs. 3. Determines the extremal structures for the isolation problem for all r≥2r \ge 2r≥2 by leveraging known results for the graph case (r=2r=2r=2).

    • thumbnail: https://pub-9ba4a5dae3bf4fc7b7d26411a74e92db.r2.dev/thumbnails/23c6d6e818480a0e14c8485fedb1fee12d1eee7d3e68d7ce778fd7fd93355ae2_w640_q70.webp

    • Simple LLM Summary: This paper addresses the problem of finding the smallest set of vertices whose closed neighborhood intersects all copies of a complete rrr-uniform hypergraph KkrK_k^rKkr​ within a connected host hypergraph. The main result proves that this isolation number is at most n/(k+1)n/(k+1)n/(k+1), with precisely characterized exceptions, thereby solving a recent conjecture. The proof builds upon the known graph case result to establish the general hypergraph bound and its extremal structures.

  • [arXiv260105] Towards a conjecture on long induced rainbow paths in triangle-free graphs

    • tags: [other], [graph theory], [triangle-free graphs, induced path, rainbow path, chromatic number, proper coloring]

    • authors: N.R. Aravind, Shiwali Gupta, Rogers Mathew

    • institution: Indian Institute of Technology Hyderabad

    • link: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2601.00602

    • contributions: 1. Improved the lower bound on the length of an induced rainbow path in a properly colored triangle-free graph from (log log log k)^(1/3 - o(1)) to (log k)^(1/2 - o(1)) vertices. 2. Proved the existence of an induced path that contains at least k/2 distinct colors in such a graph. 3. Advanced progress towards a conjecture about the existence of an induced rainbow path of length equal to the chromatic number k.

    • thumbnail: https://pub-9ba4a5dae3bf4fc7b7d26411a74e92db.r2.dev/thumbnails/41ceeeb9b644ec6e580f05d87c2f87e6bf9e6644b6cd38ca39b1743d5b1418cb_w640_q70.webp

    • Simple LLM Summary: This paper studies a conjecture about the existence of long induced rainbow paths in properly colored triangle-free graphs. The authors improve the known lower bound on the length of such a path from a triple-logarithmic function to a square-root-logarithmic function of the chromatic number. They also prove the existence of an induced path that contains at least half of the total colors.

  • [arXiv260105] Quantum Approaches to the Minimum Edge Multiway Cut Problem

    • tags: [other], [quantum optimization], [quantum annealing, QAOA, photonic computing, multiway cut, variational quantum circuits]

    • authors: Ali Abbassi, Yann Dujardin, Eric Gourdin, Philippe Lacomme, Caroline Prodhon

    • institution: Orange Research, University of Technology of Troyes (LIST3N), Université Clermont Auvergne (LIMOS)

    • link: https://arxiv.org/pdf/2601.00720

    • contributions: 1. A comparative benchmark of three distinct quantum computing paradigms (quantum annealing, gate-based QAOA, and photonic variational circuits) applied to the NP-hard Minimum Edge Multiway Cut problem. 2. An empirical assessment of the trade-offs in circuit constraints, encoding overhead, and scalability for early-stage quantum optimization on this problem class. 3. Actionable insights indicating that quantum annealing currently offers the most scalable performance for this problem, while photonic and gate-based approaches are limited by current hardware and simulation depth.

    • thumbnail: https://pub-9ba4a5dae3bf4fc7b7d26411a74e92db.r2.dev/thumbnails/57858f8028c9bc62c08c90b63c8b1671adc6ffdf9ee2c83b2de264086d39f449_w640_q70.webp

    • Simple LLM Summary: This paper benchmarks the Minimum Edge Multiway Cut problem using three quantum computing approaches: D-Wave quantum annealing, IBM's gate-based QAOA, and photonic variational circuits on Quandela's Perceval. The study evaluates their feasibility for early-stage quantum optimization, finding that quantum annealing currently provides the best scalability, while the other two methods are constrained by hardware and simulation limitations.
