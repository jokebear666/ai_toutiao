- **[arXiv260105] Quantifier Elimination Meets Treewidth**
  - **tags:** [other], [automated reasoning / formal methods], [quantifier elimination, treewidth, Fourier-Motzkin elimination, cylindrical algebraic decomposition, parameterized complexity]
  - **authors:** Hao Wu, Jiyu Zhu, Amir Kafshdar Goharshady, Jie An, Bican Xia, Naijun Zhan
  - **institution:** Institute of Software Chinese Academy of Sciences, University of Oxford, Peking University
  - **link:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2601.00312
  - **contributions:** 1. Proposes exploiting structural sparsity (via the variable dependency graph's treewidth) to mitigate the high complexity of quantifier elimination., 2. Develops a novel dynamic programming framework structured over a tree decomposition for applying FME and CAD., 3. Proves that when treewidth is constant, the framework reduces the worst-case complexity from doubly exponential to single exponential for both FME and CAD.
  - **thumbnail:** https://pub-9ba4a5dae3bf4fc7b7d26411a74e92db.r2.dev/thumbnails/3ad593b96f8b3b5a3e480c344d3dda6efcfd08788c5c383cf299ea8d548d09c4_w640_q70.webp
  - **Simple LLM Summary:** This paper addresses the high complexity of quantifier elimination (QE) procedures like Fourier-Motzkin elimination (FME) and cylindrical algebraic decomposition (CAD). It proposes a new dynamic programming framework that leverages the low treewidth of the variable dependency graph to structure the elimination process. The key result is that for constant treewidth, the method achieves an exponential improvement, reducing worst-case complexity from doubly exponential to single exponential, which is validated by preliminary experiments on sparse benchmarks.

- **[arXiv260105] Exponential lower bound via exponential sums**
  - **tags:** [other], [algebraic complexity theory], [exponential sums, algebraic circuits, Shub-Smale τ-conjecture, parameterized complexity, VNP]
  - **authors:** Somnath Bhattacharjee, Markus Bläser, Pranjal Dutta, Saswata Mukherjee
  - **institution:** University of Toronto, Saarland University, National University of Singapore
  - **link:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2601.00387
  - **contributions:** 1. Shows that the Shub-Smale τ-conjecture implies an exponential lower bound for exponential sums of algebraic circuits. 2. Proves an exponential fixed-parameter tractable (fpt) lower bound for the parameterized algebraic complexity class VW⁰_nb[P] under the same conjecture. 3. Characterizes the class VW[F] in terms of permanents, showing completeness for a specific family of polynomials derived from cycle covers.
  - **thumbnail:** https://pub-9ba4a5dae3bf4fc7b7d26411a74e92db.r2.dev/thumbnails/4dc9dd86143cd15604619616fa42b347a6ade6a21a9632b781ca7e2c99347939_w640_q70.webp
  - **Simple LLM Summary:** This paper investigates whether exponential sums of algebraic circuits inherently require exponential-size circuits. By leveraging tools from parameterized complexity, the authors prove that the Shub-Smale τ-conjecture implies such an exponential lower bound. They also establish related lower bounds for parameterized algebraic complexity classes and connect these results to collapses in the counting hierarchy.

By combining this with parameterized complexity, the authors achieve both tractability results (e.g., for problems with few distinct weights) and lower bounds (e.g., for distance to optimum), providing a more nuanced understanding of local search.

- **[arXiv260105] Stronger Approximation Guarantees for Non-Monotone γ-Weakly DR-Submodular Maximization**
  - **tags:** [ai], [submodular optimization], [weakly DR-submodular, continuous-greedy, Frank-Wolfe, approximation algorithm, down-closed convex body]
  - **authors:** Hareshkumar Jadav, Ranveer Singh, Vaneet Aggarwal
  - **institution:** IIT Indore, Purdue University
  - **link:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2601.00611

A smooth approximation guarantee that recovers the 0.401 factor for DR-submodular (γ=1) and degrades gracefully for γ&lt;1, improving upon prior bounds. 3. A hybrid algorithmic framework combining Frank-Wolfe-guided continuous-greedy with a γ-aware double-greedy step to handle non-monotonicity.

The authors propose a new algorithm that integrates a Frank-Wolfe-guided continuous-greedy approach with a γ-aware double-greedy step. This method achieves state-of-the-art approximation guarantees that depend smoothly on the parameter γ, improving upon previous results for this class of functions.
