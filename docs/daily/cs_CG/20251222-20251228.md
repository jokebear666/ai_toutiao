- **[arXiv251225] Uncovering Hierarchical Structure in LLM Embeddings with $δ$-Hyperbolicity, Ultrametricity, and Neighbor Joining**
  - **tags:** [nlp], [representation learning], [δ-hyperbolicity, ultrametricity, neighbor joining, embedding analysis, geometric group theory]
  - **authors:** Prakash Chourasia, Sarwan Ali, Murray Patterson
  - **institution:** Georgia State University, Columbia University
  - **link:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2512.20926
  - **contributions:** 1. Introduces a novel evaluation framework for LLM embeddings using three complementary geometric metrics: δ-hyperbolicity, ultrametricity, and Neighbor Joining., 2. Applies these metrics to analyze the structural properties of embeddings generated by large language models, uncovering their underlying hierarchical or tree-like organization., 3. Demonstrates that the degrees of hyperbolicity and ultrametricity in LLM embeddings correlate with their performance on downstream machine learning tasks.
  - **thumbnail:** https://pub-9ba4a5dae3bf4fc7b7d26411a74e92db.r2.dev/thumbnails/ce8f496d37ddf8620cc853b19b7132a27a2281ebfe38b32c40334e6f89b3f322_w640_q70.webp
  - **Simple LLM Summary:** This paper proposes a novel method to evaluate LLM embeddings by analyzing their geometric structure using δ-hyperbolicity, ultrametricity, and Neighbor Joining. The analysis reveals that these embeddings exhibit varying degrees of tree-like and hierarchical properties. The findings show that these structural properties correlate with the embeddings' performance on machine learning tasks.
